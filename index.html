Why the name matters

White Eagle is a documented Texas story, not a borrowed label.

In 1927 at the Feast of the Harvest Moon in Quanah, Texas, Chief Baldwin Parker, son of Quanah Parker, ceremonially honored West Texas civic leader Robert W. Haynie with Quanah Parker's war bonnet and the Comanche name White Eagle. Newspaper clippings and Haynie's own letter preserve the moment.

That history sets the tone for how this company operates: public leadership anchored in private loyalty, respect for place, and commercial relationships built through trust.
