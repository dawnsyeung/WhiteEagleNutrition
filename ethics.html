Ethics & impact

Animal wellbeing, circular agriculture, and our virtuous cycle are non-negotiable.

We believe pet wellness should never come at the expense of other animals or the natural resources we all share. Our ethically-sourced, non-GMO BSFL supply chain is developed to fulfill a virtuous cycle—rescuing nutrients, elevating animal vitality, and returning value to soils, science, and community partners from larvae to dairy herds, aquatic turtles, and every companion animal in between.

Our sustainability pillars

Each pillar protects a stage of the virtuous cycle so every formulation we ship reinforces the next: sourcing, nutrition, stewardship, and reinvestment.

Closed-loop farming

Larvae feed on upcycled produce from regional organic farms, eliminating landfill waste and returning compost to the soil.

Humane insect stewardship

We prioritize low-stress rearing conditions and rapid, humane harvesting methods in accordance with entomology standards.

Energy-positive facilities

Solar arrays and heat recapture systems power our operations, reclaiming 42% more energy than comparable poultry-based foods require.

Transparent ethical sourcing

Every batch ships with QR-coded traceability to the farms, botanicals, and third-party labs powering each formula.

Ruminant welfare alignment

Our goat, sheep, and cattle blends use copper-safe mineral ratios and resource-savvy lipid profiles vetted by large-animal veterinarians.

Digestive health & microbiome balance

BSFL is naturally rich in chitin and lauric acid, which act as prebiotic substrates and antimicrobial agents. Studies in dogs and cats demonstrate improved stool quality scores, enhanced villi height in the small intestine, and increases in beneficial bacteria such as Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium within 2-4 weeks of diet inclusion.

In ruminants, microencapsulated BSFL proteins protect key amino acids through the rumen, increasing intestinal absorption without disrupting fiber digestion. Goat and dairy cow trials report improved neutral detergent fiber digestibility, reduced rumen ammonia, and a rise in milk butterfat while maintaining stable rumen pH.

Key findings

  • In a 2022 canine trial (Journal of Animal Physiology), 10% BSFL inclusion improved stool score consistency by 21%.
  • Feline digestibility studies report >85% crude protein digestibility when BSFL replaces poultry meal.
  • Chitin fractions fermented in vitro yield short-chain fatty acids that fuel colonocytes and support barrier integrity.
  • Holstein herds fed 4% BSFL rumen-protected protein raised milk butterfat by 0.32 percentage points and improved feed efficiency (Rios et al., 2024).

Immune modulation & anti-inflammatory support

BSFL lipids are high in medium-chain fatty acids, particularly lauric acid, which exhibits antiviral and antibacterial properties. In poultry and aquaculture models, BSFL supplementation elevates immunoglobulin levels while reducing pathogen load without antibiotics.

Innate immune boost

Layer hens fed BSFL meal displayed elevated serum IgA and IgG levels alongside lower salmonella counts.

Reduced inflammation

Tilapia trials show decreased TNF-α expression and improved survival under pathogen challenge when BSFL replaces fishmeal.

Microbiota stability

In dogs, lauric acid reduces overgrowth of Clostridium perfringens while maintaining beneficial bifidobacteria.

Rumen immunity synergy

Goat kids supplemented with BSFL lipids exhibited higher IgA secretion and lowered fecal oocyst counts compared with traditional coccidiostats.

Longevity, mobility, and systemic vitality

A healthy gut and balanced immune system underpin longer health spans. BSFL contributes bioavailable calcium, phosphorus, and peptides that support joint health and metabolic efficiency, reducing the likelihood of chronic disease later in life.

  • Postbiotic peptides from BSFL fermentation modulate metabolic markers tied to healthy aging.
  • High calcium-to-phosphorus ratio strengthens skeletal systems in reptiles, turtles, and birds, preventing metabolic bone disease.
  • Functional feeding programs are evaluated for mobility and long-term performance outcomes across relevant species.
  • Essential fatty acids maintain skin integrity, reducing dermatitis-related vet visits.
  • Rumen-protected BSFL proteins elevate dairy reproductive efficiency and shorten days open by 7-10 days in peer-reviewed trials.

Pathogen-focused research collaboration

White Eagle Nutrition is collaborating with the Kansas State University Insect Farming Initiative, Symton BSF, and Entovations on pathogen-focused research evaluating the ability of black soldier fly larvae to biodigest poultry and other animal byproducts while assessing what happens to pathogen load, pathogen survivability, and microbial signatures through the larval digestion and frass production process. This research is designed to fill an important gap in the current literature: while black soldier fly larvae are widely studied for organic waste conversion, animal feed, and manure management, there is still limited applied research on their role in pathogen remediation, especially in disease-relevant animal tissue and contaminated agricultural waste streams. The project will help determine whether BSFL systems can become a practical, scalable, and sustainable tool for biosecure waste management, emergency disease response, and circular agriculture, while also generating data on the safety and biological profile of the resulting larvae and frass.

Research partnerships and study updates are published as collaborative milestones are completed.
