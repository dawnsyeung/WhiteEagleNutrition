BSFL research hub

The science powering digestive health, immunity, and longevity

This reference hub synthesizes peer-reviewed studies evaluating how black soldier fly larvae (BSFL) proteins, lipids, and fibers influence digestion, immune function, and systemic health across multiple species.

Digestive health & microbiome balance

BSFL ingredients provide highly digestible proteins alongside chitin and medium-chain fatty acids that function as fermentable substrates and antimicrobial agents.[1][2] Controlled companion-animal feeding trials report stool quality and nutrient absorption comparable to poultry-based diets while supporting beneficial lactic acid bacteria populations.[1]

Key findings

  • Companion animal diets featuring BSFL maintain amino acid digestibility on par with poultry meal formulations while preserving balanced stool consistency.[1]
  • Literature reviews highlight the role of BSFL chitin and peptides as prebiotic substrates that encourage Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium growth.[2]
  • Broiler studies note improved villus height and short-chain fatty acid production when BSFL-derived fractions appear in mixed feed matrices.[3]

Immune modulation & anti-inflammatory support

BSFL lipids supply lauric acid and other medium-chain fatty acids associated with antimicrobial and immunomodulatory effects in livestock and aquaculture trials.[2][3][4] Studies describe elevated immunoglobulin activity and pathogen resilience when BSFL meal or fat replaces conventional inputs without relying on antibiotic growth promoters.[3][4]

Innate immune boost

Broiler and layer experiments integrating BSFL meal report modulation of IgA and IgG profiles alongside stable production metrics.[3]

Reduced inflammation

Salmonid diets incorporating BSFL larvae meal maintain growth performance while supporting stress resilience during seawater transfer challenges.[4]

Microbiota stability

Medium-chain fatty acids derived from BSFL contribute to balanced gut microbial communities in monogastric livestock systems.[2]

Longevity, mobility, and systemic vitality

Long-term animal health is tied to gut integrity, metabolic balance, and nutrient density. BSFL ingredients contribute digestible amino acids, bioavailable minerals, and lipid fractions that align with these systems while supporting sustainable feed sourcing.[2][5]

  • Chitin-derived postbiotic compounds continue to be investigated for roles in metabolic regulation and oxidative balance.[2]
  • BSFL meal offers favorable calcium and phosphorus ratios for avian formulations when coupled with traditional mineral supplementation.[2]
  • Life-cycle assessments position insect-derived lipids as lower-impact alternatives to resource-intensive oils within animal nutrition supply chains.[5][6]

Species-specific data snapshots

  • Companion animals: Digestibility studies in dogs and cats show BSFL proteins can replace poultry meal without compromising nutrient uptake or stool quality.[1]
  • Poultry: Broiler and layer trials document maintained performance, gut morphology, and immune markers when BSFL meal complements or replaces conventional soybean inputs.[3]
  • Aquaculture: Atlantic salmon raised on diets containing BSFL larvae meal sustain growth performance and health metrics comparable to fishmeal-based controls.[4]
  • Feed safety and regulation: International risk assessments outline processing parameters and hazard controls required for integrating insect-derived ingredients into animal feeds.[6]
